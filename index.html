Overview

A post-war cooperative in the heart of Manhattan

408 West 34th St, also known as Madison Gardens, is a 6 story cooperative that was built in 1963. It was converted into cooperative in 1989. The building features an elevator, laundry, storage units, live-in super, and an intercom system.

There is a surface parking lot behind the coop which currently has a wait-list. The building is pet friendly and has low maintenance. Subletting is permitted, however, there is a monthly fee which is determined based off the number of years a shareholder has owned the unit.

Resident Information

Important updates and resources for shareholders

Resident Alerts & Consent

Madison Gardens offers optional SMS, email, and automated voice alerts for building-related communications (such as maintenance work, service interruptions, safety notices, and other time-sensitive updates).

How to enroll

Residents may opt in by signing the Resident Alerts Consent Form located in the building lobby. The form asks for your Name, Apartment number, Mobile phone number, and Email address.

Message frequency & opt-out

Generally up to 2 alerts per month. You may opt out at any time by replying STOP to a text message, or by emailing the board.
