Overview

Your home in Chelsea/Hudson Yards

Located in the vibrant Hudson Yards district, 408 West 34th Street, also known as Madison Gardens Apt. Corp., offers a charming post-war cooperative experience. Built in 1961 and converted to a cooperative in 1989, this six-story elevator building embodies a mid-century modern aesthetic.

Madison Gardens features 68 residences ranging from studios to spacious two-bedroom apartments. High ceilings and large windows create light-filled living spaces. The building is pet-friendly and permits subletting per board policy.

Resident Information

Important updates and resources for shareholders

Resident Alerts & Consent

Madison Gardens offers optional SMS, email, and automated voice alerts for building-related communications (such as maintenance work, service interruptions, safety notices, and other time-sensitive updates).

How to enroll

Residents may opt in by signing the Resident Alerts Consent Form located in the building lobby. The form asks for your name, apartment number, mobile phone number, and email address.

Message frequency & opt-out

Generally up to 2 alerts per month. You may opt out at any time by replying STOP to a text message, or by emailing the board.

Important: Message and data rates may apply. Reply HELP for help or STOP to unsubscribe at any time. By opting in, you agree to our Terms of Service and Privacy Policy.

Prime Hudson Yards Location

The prime location in Hudson Yards places residents at the center of a vibrant and rapidly developing neighborhood. The area is experiencing significant development, including the new 7 train station at 34th Street between 10th and 11th Avenues, the Hudson Yards project, and the Manhattan West development between 31st and 33rd Street and 9th Avenue. The neighborhood offers a plethora of high-end shops, renowned restaurants, and cultural attractions like The Shed, a contemporary arts center. Transportation options include the A/C/E, 1/2/3, 7/N/Q/R subway lines at nearby stations, plus Penn Station and Moynihan Train Hall, major hubs offering access to Amtrak, LIRR, and NJ Transit. High Line Park, a public park built on a historic freight rail line, is a short distance away.
