Overview

A post-war cooperative in the heart of Manhattan

Nestled in the heart of the redefined Hudson Yards district, 408 West 34th Street, also known as Madison Gardens, offers a charming and amenity-rich post-war cooperative experience. Built in 1963 and converted to a cooperative in 1989, this six-story elevator building embodies a mid-century modern aesthetic.

Madison Gardens boasts 68 residences, offering a comfortable range of studios, one-bedroom, and a few expansive two-bedroom apartments. High ceilings and large windows grace many units, creating a light and airy feel. While some original details might be present, most apartments offer a blend of classic features and modern updates, ensuring comfortable living spaces. Select units may feature private balconies or patios, extending the living space outdoors.

Beyond the residences, Madison Gardens provides amenities designed to enhance residents' lives. A live-in superintendent ensures the smooth operation of the building. Additional conveniences include a central laundry room, storage units, bike storage, and a surface parking lot behind the co-op (currently with a wait-list). The building is pet-friendly, and subletting is permitted per board policy.

The building sits within an area experiencing significant development, including the new 7 train station at 34th Street between 10th and 11th Avenues, the Hudson Yards project, the Manhattan West development between 31st and 33rd Street and 9th Avenue, and the relocation of Amtrak to the Farley Post Office at 33rd and 9th.

Resident Information

Important updates and resources for shareholders

Resident Alerts & Consent

Madison Gardens offers optional SMS, email, and automated voice alerts for building-related communications (such as maintenance work, service interruptions, safety notices, and other time-sensitive updates).

How to enroll

Residents may opt in by signing the Resident Alerts Consent Form located in the building lobby. The form asks for your Name, Apartment number, Mobile phone number, and Email address.

Message frequency & opt-out

Generally up to 2 alerts per month. You may opt out at any time by replying STOP to a text message, or by emailing the board.

Prime Hudson Yards Location

The prime location in Hudson Yards places residents at the center of a vibrant and rapidly developing neighborhood. The area offers a plethora of high-end shops, renowned restaurants, and cultural attractions like The Shed, a contemporary arts center. Transportation options include the A/C/E, 1/2/3, 7/N/Q/R subway lines at nearby stations, and Penn Station, a major hub offering access to Amtrak and NJ Transit. High Line Park, a public park built on a historic freight rail line, is a short distance away.
